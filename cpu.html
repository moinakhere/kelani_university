CPU

CPU stands for Central Processing Unit, which is the primary component of a computer that performs most of the processing inside a computer.

It is often referred to as the brain of the computer, as it carries out instructions from programs by performing basic arithmetic, logic, control, and input/output operations specified by the instructions.

Modern CPUs are highly complex and can contain billions of transistors, allowing them to perform billions of calculations per second.

CPUs are typically housed in a single chip and are designed to execute a wide range of tasks, from simple calculations to complex simulations.

They are essential for running operating systems and applications, making them a critical component of any computing device, including desktops, laptops, and servers.

In addition to the main CPU, many systems also include additional processors such as graphics processing units (GPUs) that handle specific tasks like rendering images and video, enhancing overall performance.

Advancements in CPU technology continue to drive improvements in computing power, energy efficiency, and overall system performance, making them a key focus in the development of new computing technologies.
